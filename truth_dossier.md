## Findings

### Question 2: What is GDOF Cell 90?
**Answer:** "GDOF Cell 90" refers to a specific "protected cell" within the **Global Dynamic Opportunities Fund (GDOF)** based in Bermuda. This cell invested 100% of its assets into **IPE Plus Fund 1** in Mauritius. 
**Key Finding:** Hindenburg documents show that the Buchs had a stake in this exact cell. This is the *same* fund structure allegedly used by **Vinod Adani** to round-trip money into Indian stocks.
**Source:** Hindenburg Research Report (Aug 2024) / IIFL Wealth Defense Statements.

### Question 9: The "Unified Code" (The Smoking Gun)
**Answer:** In **December 2025**, SEBI proposed the **"Securities Markets Code 2025 Bill"**. Crucially, it introduced **mandatory family disclosures** for board members and gave the government power to **remove officials** for conflicts of interest.
**Analysis:** This is a tacit admission that the previous rules (under which Buch operated) were insufficient. They fixed the "glitch" only *after* the scandal.
**Source:** LiveMint / Economic Times (Dec 2025).

### Question 6: The Lokpal Dismissal (May 2025)
**Answer:** The Lokpal dismissed the complaints on **May 28, 2025**, calling them "speculative" and "bordering on frivolity."
**Key Logic:** The Lokpal stated the allegations were based on "presumptions" and lacked "verifiable material." This created a legal loop: The Lokpal wouldn't investigate without proof, but proof couldn't be obtained without an investigation.
**Source:** LiveLaw, "Lokpal Order on Madhabi Buch".

### Question 14: IIFL Global's Role
**Answer:** IIFL Wealth (now 360 One WAM) managed the IPE Plus Fund 1. They defended the Buchs by stating the fund was "discretionary"—meaning investors (like the Buchs) had no say in *where* the money was invested (i.e., they couldn't *choose* Adani stocks specifically).
**Contradiction:** Hindenburg alleges Dhaval Buch wrote a letter explicitly asking to be the "sole operator," suggesting active engagement, not passive investment.

### Question 20: The JPC Demand
